Model evaluation

WHAT IF/WHATCHECK

Simple homology modelling - demo

(originally thanks to Martin Potock√Ω, much evolved since)

  • Find at least one, better two structures of any proteins PH domain and get corresponding PDB files (use e.g. NCBI Conserved Domains search with the keywords "pleckstrin homology" ... unfortunately, the search is case-insensitive, follow the Structure link and find your way among the structures with the aid of the right-hand tree).
  • Examine the structures in your browser (with the Cn3D plug-in installed) or in SPDV. Focus on structure similarity.
  • Find structural homologs for PH domain of AtPLDzeta1(see Lesson 5, sequence here) with the Fold Prediction server PHYRE (local copy of results here) . Examine the results.
  • The best PHYRE model may be already good enough; however, for training purposes we shall also try another method. Download more than one homologue structures with best E-values as PDB files - they will serve as templates for our model.
  • Open the template structures in SPDV and superimpose them with the "Iterative Magic Fit" tool from the "Fit" menu. (They may or may not fit depending what you chose - if they do not fit, choose one to proceed).
  • Choose the "Load Raw Sequence to Model" item of the "SwissModel" Menu to load the file containing fasta file with AtPLDz1 PH domain.
    The protein is built as a long perfect alpha helix.
  • Thread the sequence on the template(s) using tools in "Fit"  menu.
  • Make sure the current layer is AtPLDz1_PH, and click on the little black arrow located at the right of the question mark of the Align Window. The window expands, and displays a curve depicting how each residue likes its surrounding. If a residue is "happy", its energy is below zero, whereas an unhappy residue will have an energy above the zero axis. This is the mean force potential energy.
  • Now you can play with the alignment to get lower energy and acceptable bond lengths.
  • You can evaluate how good your threading is by using the "select aa making clashes" items of the "Select" menu. This will allow you to quickly focus on potentially problematic regions. You can then choose the "Fix Selected Sidechains" item of the "Tools" menu, which will browse the rotamer library to choose the best rotamer (use the "quick and dirty" method to avoid crashing the workstation).
  • When everything seems okay, you can submit a modelling request to Swiss-Model simply by choosing the "Submit modelling request" of the "Swiss-model" menu, assuming that you have registered there. By default, you will get a Swiss-PdbViewer project file, with your model aligned onto the templates you used, and ready for comparison. The project comes back at the e-mail address you have provided. as a PDB-formatted file.
  • Inspect output of evaluation of WhatCheck software generated for your model (for an example look here).
  • Play with the problematic regions and then resubmit the model. Repeat until the model is perfect or until you smash your computer (more often).
